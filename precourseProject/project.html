A Glimpse into my Life

list of favorite places i've been to with images

  1. St. Marks Basilica: Venice Italy

    2. I couldn't find a single image to do this magnificent work of architecture justice. the interior has stunning mosaics made from breathtaking colors. The doge palace is connected to the basilica which contains royal apartments, the bridge of sighs, and even a very old prison with limestone walls. There is a massive clocktower in st. Marks square and there are thousands of pigeons that will eat the bread straight out of your hand roaming around the grounds.

  2. StoneHenge: Wiltshire England

    3. In my free time I enjoy studying the mysteries of life. One of them to ponder is StoneHenge. The geology of the area suggests the rocks were most closely found in Northern Africa and transported all the way to StoneHenge. The site was constructed roughly 5000 years ago when moving these massive rocks was nearly impossible with their technology. The constructors were the high priests of the Celts, who constructed it for sacrificial ceremonies. Other people arugue that the druids built it for studying astronomy. The site is SURROUNDED by burrial mounds, and is located almost perfectly on the prime meridian. seems too complex for people who were alive at the time and still remains an enigma to a lot of people today.

  3. Napali Coast: Kawaii Hawaii

    4. I love this image because it shows the most dangerous beach in the world. The beach has claimed over 150 lives of the unsuspecting people who get pulled into the very strong riptide. It is highly discouraged to allow the water to reach your knees.

  4. Alcova Wyoming

    5. One of my hobbies inclues fishing. This is probably the best rainbow trout fishing spot I have ever been to. If you go during the correct season the water below the dam where the trout go to spawn will be running at a manageable pace to flyfish narrow streams. Once you find the "honey hole" and the correct fly you will be picking them out of the river like a sushi buffet.

  5. Top of Bear Peak

    6. as much as I enjoy hiking the flatirons I feel I need to do it more often. It's such a blessing having a 3-5 hour hike to a beautiful peak right in your back yard. I usually save the occasion for when the stresses of life finally get to me and I feel like i'm about to explode. The feeling of looking down and being able to see where you were born and where you have lived your entire life right in front of you all in one panoramic veiw strikes up a lot of thought. I have found myself sitting at the top until the very last moment so I don't have to hike back down in the dark. I Was born in Denver Colorado, raised in Lousiville Colorado, went to college at CU Boulder, reside in Lafayette and Superior Colorado. therefore I feel a deep connection with the flatirons having been around them my entire life.
